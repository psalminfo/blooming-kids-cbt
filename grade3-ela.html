Passage 1: The Little Red Hen

Once upon a time, a Little Red Hen lived on a farm with a dog, a cat, and a pig. One day, she found some wheat seeds. She decided to plant them to make bread. "Who will help me plant these seeds?" she asked. The dog said, "Not I." The cat said, "Not I." The pig said, "Not I." So, the Little Red Hen planted the seeds herself. When the wheat grew, she asked, "Who will help me cut the wheat?" Again, the dog, cat, and pig said, "Not I." So, she cut it herself. Later, she asked, "Who will help me grind the wheat into flour?" and "Who will help me bake the bread?" Each time, her friends refused, and she did the work alone. Finally, the bread was ready, and it smelled delicious. "Who will help me eat the bread?" she asked. The dog, cat, and pig shouted, "I will!" But the Little Red Hen said, "I worked hard alone, so I will eat it myself." And she did.

1. What did the Little Red Hen want to make with the wheat seeds?

2. Why did the Little Red Hen do all the work herself?

3. What is the main idea of the story?

4. How did the Little Red Hen feel when her friends wanted to eat the bread?

5. What does the word "grind" mean in the story?

Passage 2: A Day at the Park

Sofia and her brother Leo went to the park on a sunny Saturday. They ran to the playground, where swings and slides sparkled under the sun. Sofia loved the swings. She pumped her legs and soared high, feeling like a bird. Leo climbed the slide and slid down with a big smile. After playing, they sat under a big oak tree to eat their picnic. Their mom packed sandwiches, apples, and juice. A squirrel scampered nearby, watching them eat. Sofia tossed it a tiny piece of bread, and it grabbed it and ran up the tree. Later, they played tag, running across the grassy field until they were tired. As the sun set, they walked home, talking about their fun day.
